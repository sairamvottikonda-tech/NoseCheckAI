# Photo Guidelines for Accurate NoseCheck Analysis

## Optimal Camera Angle

### ✅ CORRECT: Frontal View (Eye Level)

```
     [Camera]
        |
        | 0° (horizontal)
        |
        ↓
    [Your Face]
```

- Camera at **same height as your eyes**
- Look **straight into camera**
- Phone/camera held **vertically** (not tilted)
- Distance: **arm's length** (50-70cm)

### ❌ INCORRECT: Upward Angle (~35°)

```
     [Your Face]
        ↑
       /  35° upward tilt
      /
 [Camera]
```

**Problems:**
- Nose appears shorter (foreshortening)
- Nostrils overly visible
- Septal angle compressed
- **Reduces accuracy by 20-40%**

### ❌ INCORRECT: Downward Angle

```
 [Camera]
      \
       \  downward tilt
        ↓
    [Your Face]
```

**Problems:**
- Nose appears elongated
- Bridge emphasized, tip hidden
- Less accurate measurements

## Complete Photo Checklist

### 📱 Camera Setup
- [ ] Phone/camera at **eye level**
- [ ] **Arm's length** distance (50-70cm)
- [ ] Camera **perpendicular** to face (not tilted)
- [ ] Use **portrait orientation** (vertical)

### 💡 Lighting
- [ ] **Even, soft lighting** (avoid harsh shadows)
- [ ] Light from **front or sides** (not from above/below)
- [ ] Avoid **direct sunlight** (causes overexposure)
- [ ] Indoor lighting or **overcast outdoor** light ideal

### 🎭 Expression
- [ ] **NEUTRAL expression** (no smile, no frown)
- [ ] Mouth **closed naturally**
- [ ] Face **relaxed**
- [ ] Look **directly at camera**

### 🎯 Framing
- [ ] Face fills **60-80% of frame**
- [ ] **Entire nose visible** (tip, bridge, nostrils)
- [ ] Eyes visible (for angle detection)
- [ ] No obstructions (glasses OK, but remove if thick frames)

### 🚫 What to Avoid
- ❌ Smiling or laughing
- ❌ Looking up or down
- ❌ Head tilted sideways
- ❌ Harsh shadows on one side
- ❌ Overexposed (too bright) or underexposed (too dark)
- ❌ Motion blur
- ❌ Extreme close-up or far away

## Example Setup

### Good Setup:
1. **Stand near window** (for natural light) or in well-lit room
2. **Hold phone at eye level**, arm's length away
3. **Look directly at camera**
4. **Keep face neutral** (relax jaw, no expression)
5. **Take 3-5 photos** for best results
6. **Review** - face should be centered, well-lit, and clear

### If Using Someone Else's Help:
1. Ask them to hold camera at **your eye level**
2. Stand **directly facing them**, not at angle
3. Camera should be **50-70cm away**
4. Ask for **neutral expression**
5. Take **multiple shots** to choose best

## For 3D Model Calibration

### Model Positioning:
- Mount model on **stable stand**
- Position at **camera height** (eye level equivalent)
- Ensure model is **perpendicular** to camera
- Use **tripod** for consistency across all models

### Consistency Checklist:
- [ ] Same distance for all models (measure!)
- [ ] Same lighting setup
- [ ] Same camera settings (auto or manual - but consistent)
- [ ] Same background
- [ ] Model orientation identical (mark base position)

### Quality Check:
Take test photo, then verify:
- Model nose fills appropriate frame percentage
- No glare or reflections on model
- Sharp focus on nose area
- Even lighting (no harsh shadows)

If angle detection shows **Quality Score < 70%**, retake photo!

## Camera Angle Quality Scores

The algorithm automatically detects camera angle:

| Quality Score | Meaning | Action |
|---------------|---------|--------|
| **90-100%** | Perfect frontal | ✅ Use as-is |
| **70-89%** | Slight tilt | ⚠️ Acceptable, but retake if possible |
| **50-69%** | Moderate tilt | ⚠️ Measurements compensated, retake recommended |
| **< 50%** | Severe tilt | ❌ Retake required for accuracy |

## Tips for Smartphone Users

### iPhone/iOS:
- Use **Portrait mode** if available (better face focus)
- Tap on nose to **focus**
- Use **timer** (3-second) for steadier shot
- Grid lines: Settings → Camera → Grid (helps with alignment)

### Android:
- Enable **grid overlay** in camera settings
- Use **Pro mode** for manual control if needed
- Tap to focus on nose
- Use timer for stability

### Both:
- **Clean camera lens** before shooting
- **Hold phone steady** or use tripod
- Take **multiple photos**, pick best
- Review immediately and retake if needed

## Why Angle Matters

### Impact on Measurements:

| Camera Angle | Effect on Nose | Measurement Impact |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Frontal (0°)** | True proportions | ✅ Accurate |
| **Upward (30-40°)** | Appears shorter, nostrils prominent | ❌ Under-scores deviation by 20-40% |
| **Downward** | Appears longer, tip hidden | ❌ Over-emphasizes bridge |
| **Sideways** | Asymmetric view | ❌ Cannot measure accurately |

**Bottom line:** Even a "good looking" photo at the wrong angle will give inaccurate results. **Eye-level frontal view is essential.**
