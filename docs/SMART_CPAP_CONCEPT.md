# Smart CPAP/Mask for Nasal Issues — Concept Document

## 1. Problem Statement

**Who is affected?**
- People with nasal obstruction (deviated septum, allergies, congestion)
- CPAP users who struggle with mask fit or pressure
- Anyone whose sleep is disrupted by blocked nasal passages

**What’s the pain?**
- Standard CPAP uses fixed pressure; it doesn’t adapt to changing nasal blockage
- Users don’t know if their breathing is efficient during the night
- No real-time feedback to adjust position, pressure, or airflow
- Trial-and-error with settings can take weeks

---

## 2. Solution (Core Idea)

**What we’re building:**
A small prototype mask that:
1. **Tracks** breathing efficiency in real time
2. **Gives feedback** (visual, sound, or haptic) when airflow is poor
3. **Optionally adjusts** airflow or pressure in response to blockage

**How it’s different:**
- Not just monitoring — it responds in real time
- Focus on nasal obstruction, not only sleep apnea
- Can work as a standalone device or with existing CPAP

---

## 3. Target User

**Primary:** Adults with nasal obstruction who have trouble sleeping  
- May or may not use CPAP  
- Want to understand and improve their breathing at night  

**Secondary:** CPAP users who feel their current setup doesn’t adapt well to congestion or position changes  

---

## 4. Main Feature (MVP)

**Minimum viable product:**
A mask that:
- Measures airflow or pressure at the nose
- Detects when breathing is inefficient (e.g., mouth breathing, high resistance)
- Sends a simple alert (e.g., vibration or tone) to prompt the user to change position or clear the nose

**Not in MVP:**
- Automatic pressure adjustment
- Full CPAP integration
- Cloud analytics

---

## 5. Success Criteria

- User can wear the mask and sleep
- Device detects at least one type of breathing problem (e.g., mouth breathing vs. nasal)
- User receives clear, non-intrusive feedback
- User reports improved awareness of breathing quality

---

## 6. Connection to NoseCheck

| NoseCheck | Smart Mask |

| Photo + questionnaire | Real-time sensors |
| One-time or periodic check | Continuous monitoring |

**Integration idea:** NoseCheck score could suggest when to try the smart mask or which settings to start with.
