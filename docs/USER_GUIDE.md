# NoseCheck User Guide

## Understanding Your Results

### Deviation Score (0-100)

Higher score = more asymmetry detected

- **0-25 (Normal):** Minimal asymmetry. Most people have some minor variation - this is normal.
- **25-45 (Mild):** Slight asymmetry detected. Monitor symptoms. Consider ENT if symptoms develop.
- **45-65 (Moderate):** Noticeable asymmetry. Recommend ENT evaluation, especially with symptoms.
- **65-100 (Severe):** Significant asymmetry detected. Strongly recommend ENT consultation.

### Important Notes

**This is a screening tool, not a diagnosis.**
- Photos alone may not capture internal septal deviation
- Algorithm is **intentionally sensitive** - better to flag and check than miss problems
- False positives are possible (normal noses flagged as mild/moderate)
- Combined visual + symptom score is more reliable than photo alone

### When to See an ENT

Consider an ENT evaluation if:
- Score is moderate or higher (45+)
- You have nasal obstruction, mouth breathing, or sleep issues
- Even if score is normal but symptoms are significant
- Score is mild but symptoms are worsening

### Photo Tips for Accurate Results

- Use **frontal face view** (straight on, not angled)
- **Neutral expression** (no smile - changes facial geometry)
- **Good even lighting** (avoid harsh shadows or overexposure)
- **Fill the frame** (face should be clearly visible)
- Remove glasses, hats, or anything covering the nose

### Understanding the Metrics

The algorithm measures:

1. **Lateral Deviation:** Distance of nose tip from facial midline
2. **Septal Angle:** How much the bridge-to-tip line tilts from vertical
3. **Nostril Asymmetry:** Difference in left vs right nostril size
4. **Bridge Straightness:** Deviation of nasal bridge from midline

### Symptom Questionnaire

The symptom questionnaire is **critical** for accurate screening:
- Symptoms account for 40% of combined score
- Many people with septal deviation have symptoms even if external appearance is normal
- Be honest about symptom severity (0=none, 1=mild, 2=moderate, 3=severe)

### Limitations

**Cannot detect:**
- Internal septal spurs or deviations not visible externally
- Turbinate hypertrophy
- Nasal polyps or other internal obstructions
- Functional issues vs structural

**Always consult a qualified ENT for:**
- Persistent nasal symptoms
- Breathing difficulties
- Sleep issues
- Any concerns about nasal health
